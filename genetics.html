Genetics of Mental Disorders

Interdisciplinary study combining genetics and mental health data.

Longer description and dataset information; include links to ethical approvals and collaborators. This project focused on rare protein-truncating variants (PTVs), which can severely disrupt gene function, and applied a statistical framework called Burden Heritability Regression to estimate how much these rare variants contribute to disease risk and to examine genetic overlap across disorders. The results suggest that distinct brain disorders may partially arise from common disrupted biological pathways. While this work focuses on population-level genetic patterns, future research will extend these results to a single-case (N-of-1) study in autism by combining genetic data with detailed behavioral, cognitive, and longitudinal measurements. This next step aims to connect large-scale genetic discoveries to the biology and behavior of an individual, helping bridge the gap between big-data genetics and personalized understanding of brain disorders.
